01 Catalon ▶ live demo AI automation for chemical commercial teams. Purchase-order processing, ~40 min → ~3 sec.

As technical cofounder I built the agentic pipeline behind Catalon. It reads an inbound purchase-order email, classifies intent, resolves the customer against their ERP, extracts and price-validates every line item — chemical-synonym resolution, density-based lbs↔gal conversion, customer-specific rules — and drafts an ERP-ready order for one-click human approval. Sub-3-second latency, sitting on top of existing systems with no rip-and-replace and no retraining.

  • Multi-stage state machine: deterministic prefilter (~15% never hit the LLM) → LLM classify → hybrid SQL+LLM customer-resolve → schema-driven extraction → self-healing retry — with a circuit breaker, token-bucket rate limiter, exponential backoff, audit trail and dead-letter queue for production resilience.
  • Schema-decoupled: a reusable inference core with hot-swappable per-customer “context cartridges” (prompts, schemas, rules, domain reference). A new deployment is eval-tune-and-swap, not a rewrite — eval-driven tuning, not fine-tuning → 99%+ accuracy.
  • 2 paying distributors, $60K ARR and a $1.2M pipeline within 2 months — then exited.

02 PourDynamics A multiscale physics engine mapping brewing variables to coffee flavour.

A 2D-axisymmetric Doyle–Fuller–Newman model ported from lithium-ion battery electrochemistry — coffee extraction and a battery are, mathematically, the same equations.

  • Particle-scale diffusion coupled to a bed-scale advection–dispersion–extraction PDE; validated against Crank’s analytical sphere solution (sub-0.2%).
  • Sensory layer: dose-over-threshold → Weber–Fechner compression → taste-weight mix → cross-modal masking, turning concentrations into perceived taste.
  • The prize is J = dy/dx — the Jacobian of which knobs move flavour. Strategy: open the method, close the product.

03 3D-Software-MCP-Server ▶ live demo Natural language → 3D seismic navigation inside pro software “Tornado”.

Professional seismic-interpretation software has a brutal learning curve, and expert time is scarce. I wrapped it in an agent: you describe what you want to see — “go to the gas area, rotate, toggle the velocity model” — and an LLM turns it into live, validated commands against the application’s API. Like having a seismologist drive the software next to you, available around the clock.

  • Natural language → JSON-RPC via an LLM, gated by a command validator (only whitelisted function calls execute), with multi-LLM fallback, a SQLite/Firebase command queue, and a listener that applies commands to the software one at a time.
  • 20-level undo/redo over view state; a bookmark engine reading/writing the software’s view XML; a bidirectional coordinate transform between seismic (inline / crossline / depth) and Cartesian space.
  • Built solo in ~3 weeks at Viridien on my own initiative; drew interest from teams across China, the Netherlands, the US and Singapore.

05 FlowR (MellowDrip) A physics-informed smart coffee brewer.

Modelling immersion vs percolation (Noyes–Whitney diffusion) showed only two variables really matter: flow rate and grind size. So I built hardware to control them.

  • Measures output flow rate via the differential of two scales, feeding a PID loop on an ESP8266 (C++, real-time) that controls input flow.
  • FlowR v1 proved the concept; v2 added aesthetics and a Blynk IoT app. Showcased to 150 people.

08 iGEM 2025 (Oxford) ▶ live demo Gold medal — high-performance CRISPR guide discovery.

Finding CRISPR guides that hit a target bacterium while sparing the commensal flora is, underneath, a nearest-neighbour search over millions of genomic sites by Hamming distance. Done the obvious way it is O(mn) and takes years. I reframed it as a search-structure problem and engineered it down to minutes.

  • BK-trees for Hamming-distance nearest-neighbour search; tries truncated to the first checked bases to slash memory; bitwise genome masking; Cython inner loops, CUDA-batched lookups and shared-memory parallelism across processes.
  • Complexity O(mn) → O(m·log n); 55M base pairs in under 30 seconds. ML & algorithm lead; represented Oxford at iGEM 2025.

▸ Battery State-of-Health ▶ live demo Recovering how a cell degraded — from its voltage curves, without opening it.

Imperial College London · Faraday Institution · ongoing publication

A lithium-ion cell’s health is usually read by tearing it down. I treat it instead as an inverse problem: reconstruct the full-cell open-circuit-voltage curve from its cathode and anode contributions, and the way they have shifted tells you exactly which electrode lost capacity and why — non-invasively. The catch is that the standard fit misbehaves for NMC811–graphite, returning spurious, even negative, degradation modes. The root cause I found: scipy.curve_fit returns a local minimum, heavily dependent on the initial stoichiometric parameters.

  • Differential Evolution for a true global fit — least-squares 0.0004 vs 0.005, and OCV fits that actually track the data.
  • DV_fit: differential-voltage (dV/dQ) feature-matching via optimised Dynamic Time Warping — peak/dip locations are invariant in lithiation even as their shape changes, so they anchor the fit.
  • Degraded_OCV_fit: uses DV_fit’s predicted degraded cathode OCPs over a Pchip-DTW degradation manifold — the most consistent modes so far. NMC811–graphite, 41 cells; awaiting experimental verification.
